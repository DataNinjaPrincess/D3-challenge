Correlation Between Poverty and Healthcare, by State

Correlations Discovered Between Poverty Rate and Lack of Healthcare

Utilizing the US Census Bureau's 2014 ACS 1-year estimates, we can note a positive correlation between poverty rates and lack of health care across the US. For example, Massachussets has a relatively low poverty rate at 11.6% and an equally low percent of the population lacking healthcare at 4.6%. On the other end, Texas is on the higher end of the poverty rate at 17.2%, and has 24.9% of its population lacking healthcare.
